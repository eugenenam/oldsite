My Story

After graduating from the University of Notre Dame in 2007 with a B.B.A. in Finance, I began my career at ABN AMRO N.V. as an Analyst in the Credit Portfolio Management group. With the acquisition of ABN by RBS Americas in 2008, I continued my banking career in Stamford, Connecticut as Risk Associate, covering large corporates such in the Consumer Products and Casino Gaming Sector. After moving back to NYC for a Risk Management role at the RBC Capital Markets, I worked my way to an Associate Director / Vice President Role in the Risk Management group for Leverage Finance. In this role, I managed bank exposure of ~$1.0Bn across 25 clients within the Communications, Media, and Entertainment portfolio.

While having found success in the corporate world, I realized that my growth in banking was stagnating and needed to make a career change. In 2013, I decided to step outside of my norm and enroll in a Product Management course at General Assembly. Being surrounded by lifelong learners and ambitious creators, I knew I made the right decision to explore outside of banking and finance. In 2014, I continued my education in technology with UX Design under the guidance of Lauren Bugeja (UX Lead at Google). And in 2015, I officially retired from banking/finance and enrolled full-time at the Startup Institute.

My ongoing journey into technology and startups has connected me with talented and inspiring folks and I am looking forward to the chapter in my life.

Why Startups?

I'm in awe of all the amazing things happening in the startup community, and I want in. I want to do more than just earn a paycheck. I want to be a part of something new and exciting, something that will make a difference in my life and the lives of others. I want to work with like-minded people who love what they do and the company they work for. I want to be part of a company whose values I believe in, one that promotes and encourages creativity, innovation, and collaboration. I want to be challenged, and to be able to learn and grow outside of the confines of a standard job title or description.

Working in the corporate space for so many years, I felt boxed in and found that the culture did not allow me to create my own path. That's why startup culture really appeals to me. I want to work for a company that allow me to be creative and tap into my full potential, and where my work will directly contribute to the growth of the company.
